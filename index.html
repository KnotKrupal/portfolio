About

A generalist who actually ships things

I started in computer engineering, fell in love with VFX, and now live where software, machines and people meet. My favourite problems sit on factory floors and print rooms – with cables, noise, and real deadlines.

I currently work at St. Regis Group in the Toronto area, operating and gently improving a fleet of Mimaki UV printers, a Canon Arizona, an OMTech MOPA fiber laser and a growing collection of jigs, fixtures and internal tools.

My toolkit ranges from Python, PowerShell and n8n to Bambu Lab X1E prints, camera-ready VFX shots, and quiet little process changes that help operators and managers breathe easier.

I care about repeatability, clarity, and leaving behind systems instead of personal heroics.

Things I’ve built or shaped

A few representative projects. Some are deployed on a busy shop floor, some are in active prototype, and some are concept pieces that keep me learning.

SmartImprint-style Artwork Manager

Python · Production tool

A Python GUI that searches, validates and routes EPS/CDR artwork from complex network folders into the correct print queues for different order types.

  • Python
  • Tkinter
  • File automation
  • Production support

Problem. Operators were losing time hunting for files across deep folder structures and sometimes printing from the wrong revision.

What I did. Designed a small GUI tool to index likely artwork folders, search by work order, validate expected file patterns, and move the right assets into hot folders for Mimaki print queues.

Impact. Turned “ask three people and check six folders” into a consistent 1–2 click flow. Easier to train, easier to audit, and much kinder under deadline.

Pen laser engraving cell – single-pen flow

Automation concept · 3D print

A compact pen engraving cell around an OMTech MOPA fiber laser, using a rotary chuck, guides, and a future Arduino/ESP32 control path.

  • Mechanism design
  • Fixtures
  • Automation concept

Problem. Pen engraving was reliable but manual – each pen was aligned by hand, with limited throughput and fatigue risk.

What I did. Sketched and 3D-printed a series of guides, ramps and chuck interfaces for a single-pen workflow, with clear reference datums and a future path to motorised feeding.

Impact. Gave a concrete blueprint for automation: a path from “one careful operator” to a stable, repeatable cell ready for sensors and motion control.

Fixture library for bottles & odd shapes

Bambu X1E · Shop-floor support

A growing library of low-profile jigs for cylindrical and irregular products, tuned for tight Z-clearance on UV and laser setups.

  • 3D printing
  • Parametric design
  • Shop support

Problem. Every new product required a fresh improvisation to hold it safely and repeatably under the print head.

What I did. Built and refined a set of reusable base patterns for jigs that keep most of the geometry generic and only swap out a few key dimensions.

Impact. Reduced the mental load of “how do we hold this?” and made it realistic to keep adding new SKUs without losing setup sanity.

Thin client & terminal locator

PowerShell · Data

Scripts and a lightweight convention to map remote terminals to their actual physical locations on the shop floor.

  • PowerShell
  • CSV/JSON
  • IT collaboration

Problem. IT knew the devices. Production knew the desks. No one had a simple, shared view of which was which.

What I did. Built a script to collect consistent IDs and exported them into a format that matched how the physical spaces were described.

Impact. Gave IT and production a shared language for “that terminal over there,” making support and planning smoother.
